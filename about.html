Mission & Vision/Why SciComm


At Sci4Teens, we realize that many teens across the globe are passionate about STEM, but need futher guidance on how to communicate their research or their understanding of a certain topic. This is why Sci4Teens creates equal opportunities for students looking to pursue STEM, humanities, and science communication (SciComm) through our contests (involving writing, art, infographics, and more), workshops, and newsletter. We hope that through this organization, more teens will understand the importance of communicating their work, and can apply these skills in future careers.

FAQ


You may work either individually or in teams, with the maximum team size being three people.

Your piece can be a blog-style article about COVID-19 effects, vaccine development, or crisis management!

The contest judges are professionals in the field of Science Communication. We aim to make the process double-blind, so that there are no conflicts of interest in the process.

Workshops will present STEM/Science Communication topics that are beneficial for both the contest and your future!

Contact us at sci4teens@gmail.com if you'd like to get interviewed, and we will respond to you with the details!

Feel free to contact a mentor - the list of mentors is within the mentors page on this website! Also, check out our recently added SciComm FAQs (on the website)!

You can send an email at sci4teens@gmail.com with your question.
